Initial Research

Problem Statement

Harmful stereotypes of the queer community in media play a role in affecting the mental health of queer youth, persisting despite the growth in representation. Certain outlets continue to perpetuate harmful stereotypes, impacting the well-being of the queer community, especially among youth.

Need Statement

Queer youth need accurate and positive representation in media to combat harmful stereotypes, fostering a sense of belonging and boosting self-esteem. Accurate portrayals aim to help improve issues like alarming statistics on bullying and violence. This is essential for creating a supportive environment and improving the mental health of queer youth.

"Two-thirds of LGBTQ youth faced attempts to change their sexual orientation, with 76% citing the political climate's impact on their mental health. Less than half were out to an adult at school, and 58% of transgender/nonbinary respondents reported discouragement from using gender-consistent bathrooms. The CDC's 2015 Youth Risk Behavior Survey revealed that 34% of LGBT youth experienced school bullying, 18% faced physical or dating violence, and the same percentage reported sexual assault. Additionally, approximately 1 in 10 faced threats or injuries with a weapon at school."
