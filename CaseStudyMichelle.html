Case Study!

Recap Of Case Study


imi is a web app that provides guides to activities, resources, communities, and learning opportunities designed for and with LGBTQ+ teens to help them explore their identities and support their mental health.

Queer youth experience worse mental health than their cis-heterosexual peers, often due to factors such as family rejection and bullying. Addressing this gap requires accessible resources for mental health support, crisis intervention, and safe spaces for connecting with other queer youth.

Most Effective Method


The most effective thing to combat worsened mental health in queer youth is to provide positive affirmation and representation.

In Volume 43 of the Clinical Social Work Journey (published by Springer) written by Gio Iacono, a professor at the University of Connecticut, evidence shows that affirmative and mindfulness approaches substantially improve the mental health of queer youth as they are more likely to experience elevated mental health risks and vulnerabilities. This looks like having queer role models and social networks/communities.

Use of Technology


Websites and social media can share helpful info and stories globally, supporting queer youth across the world and improving their mental health by providing accessible resources online.

It's crucial that digital safe spaces, like imi's website and social media, offer free and accessible support. This ensures all queer youth can navigate their mental health without barriers, overcoming isolation or judgment.

Implications For My Actual Project


Showcasing genuine stories to queer youth can enhance their perceptions of themselves which improves their mental health.

In developing my website, I'll prioritize gathering information from real queer individuals. In a text-based game, I will draw from authentic narratives to create characters that genuinely represent the queer community.
